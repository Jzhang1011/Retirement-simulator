Roth conversion sweep — optimization & ACA (simplified)

Higher final Roth does not necessarily mean higher final after-tax wealth. If the same assets earn the same return in pretax vs Roth and the effective tax rate is unchanged, timing the recognition alone does not create wealth. This tool ranks “max after-tax net worth” on the terminal after-tax sum across Roth + pretax (haircut) + taxable (embedded LTCG) + cash, not on Roth size; when two rows tie on that sum, the pick prefers lower modeled tax then a lower annual conversion target.

Optimization insights (Advanced & Advanced + flexible college)

Each sweep changes one input while holding your form values fixed. Per-year conversion JSON is ignored during sweeps. For “max after-tax net worth,” ties on terminal ATW prefer lower modeled tax and a lower conversion target — not a larger Roth. The full advanced engine is not a pure two-account tax-neutral toy: conversion tax is paid from taxable first (then a stylized IRA draw if short), while spend/college/SS interact with balances.
